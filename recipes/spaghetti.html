Spaghetti Recipe

Picture of a fresh plate of Spaghetti

Spaghetti is a classic Italian pasta dish that is loved worldwide for its simplicity and deliciousness.
The dish typically features long, thin noodles made from durum wheat, cooked al dente and served with a variety of sauces.
A popular choice is the traditional tomato sauce, made from ripe tomatoes, garlic, onions, and herbs like basil and oregano.
For a richer flavor, many recipes include ground meat or vegetables sautéed in olive oil before adding the sauce.
Spaghetti can also be served with creamy sauces like Alfredo or carbonara, which include ingredients like cream, cheese, and pancetta.
This versatile dish can be customized with various toppings such as grated Parmesan cheese, fresh herbs, or chili flakes for added heat.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti noodles
  • Tomatoes (fresh or canned)
  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Basil
  • Oregano
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Parmesan cheese (optional)

Steps

  1. Boil water in a large pot and add salt.
  2. Add spaghetti noodles and cook according to package instructions until al dente.
  3. In a separate pan, heat olive oil over medium heat.
  4. Add chopped onions and minced garlic; sauté until fragrant.
  5. Add diced tomatoes (fresh or canned) and cook until the sauce thickens.
  6. Stir in chopped basil and oregano; season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Drain the spaghetti and toss it with the sauce in the pan.
  8. Serve hot, garnished with grated Parmesan cheese if desired.
  9. Enjoy your delicious spaghetti!
